Express gratitude

When you're depressed, especially at this awful time, it can seem that everything in life is bleak and hopeless. But even in the darkest days, it's usually possible to find one thing you can be grateful about- the beauty of a sunset or a phone call from a friend, for example. It sounds chessy but acknowledging your gratitude can provide a respite from negative thinking and really boost your mood.

Maintain a routine

Sleeping too much or too little, skipping meals or exercise, and neglecting your personal care only feeds your depression. Establishing and maintaining a daily routine, on the other hand, adds structure to your day, even if you're alone and out of work. Try to include set times for exercising, spending time outside, and communicating with friends each day.

Limit your consumption of news

Yes, you want to stay informed, but overconsuming sensationalistic news or unreliable social media coverage will only fuel your negativity and fear. Limit how often you check news or social media and confine yourself to reputable sources.

Find simple sources of joy

While you can't force yourself to have fun, you can push yourself to do things that will boost your mood throughout day. Try listening to uplifting music (evn getting up and dancing around if you can) or finding a reason to laugh by watching funny videos on You Tube or episodes of your favorite sitcom. Spending time in nature- whether it's walking in the park, paddling on the beach, or going for a hike- can ease stress and put a smile on your face, even if you're alone. Or try playing with kids or a pet- they'll benefit as much as you will.
